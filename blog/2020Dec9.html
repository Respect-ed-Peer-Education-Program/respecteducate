Moving forward with the Sex(Ed) program

Dec. 9 2020 | Respect(Ed)

This past August, we announced the launch of our Sex(Ed) pilot program as a supplement to online learning for Oregon youth. Since then, we’ve heard first hand from our participants about the unique difficulties of distance learning, and we’ve been thinking hard about how to adapt our programming to be more accessible.

Moving out of the pilot period of this program, we will be expanding to offer youth an array of online leadership, community, and educational opportunities, all under the banner of the Sex(Ed) program:

  1. We will be hosting a bimonthly community discussion space facilitated by peer educators. While each month will have a sexuality-related discussion theme, the discussion space is more so a time for young people to gather virtually to seek community, support, respond to current events, and ask for guidance from peers and allies during this difficult time.
  2. Sex(Ed) peer educators and program directors will also be leading a workshop series on various sexuality topics (sexual violence, consent, healthy relationshiops, safe sex, anatomy, gender, sexuality, etc.) as selected by youth participants.
  3. All youth will have pdf access to the Sex(Ed) Guide Book, a comprehensive resource designed to accompany and supplement in-person Sex(Ed) programming. The guide covers all the sexuality topics we teach and links to additional resources. Rather than a textbook, the guide functions as a reference and a place for youth to source reliable information on consent and sexuality.
  4. Finally, youth can apply to be a part of the Respect(Ed) Leadership Institute, which includes a commitment to attend monthly discussion groups and workshops, in addition to extra advocacy and peer education trainings. Leadership participants will have the opportunity to plan workshops, develop educational resources, and gain an in-depth knowledge of consent and sexuality topics.


You can learn more about Sex(Ed) programming and how to get involved here.

Sex(Ed) programming, like all of our educational programs, is completely free of charge. We believe that accurate and inclusive sexuality education is a right, even and especially during a pandemic. It does cost money to run Sex(Ed) programming and your generous donations make our work possible.
