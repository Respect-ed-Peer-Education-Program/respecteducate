Queer Book Recommendations from the Directors

Jan. 22 2021 | Respect(Ed) Directors

During our Sex(Ed) programming this month, we’ve been discussing queer identity and queer history. As January draws to a close, we thought we’d offer you this list of recommendations — books we ourselves have found informative and affirming as young queer people. We hope you will enjoy them as much as we did.

Jade’s top picks:

Gender Failure, by Ivan Coyote & Rae Spoon

Gender Failure is a sweet nonfiction narrative of how two transmasculine people discovered their nonbinary identities and relationship to traditional gender/trans narratives and dysphoria. Their backgrounds as artists and storytellers facilitate beautiful and extremely authentic language and voice throughout the book.

The Letter Q: Queer Writers’ Notes to their Younger Selves, edited by Sarah Moon

The Letter Q is a heartwarming compilation of letters, reminiscent of the It Gets Better Project. It is a sweet read as an adult queer person and a deeply affirming and assuring book for young queer people discovering their identities. My only complaint is that this book includes no transgender authors.

American Boys, by Soraya Zaman

American Boys is an incredible compilation of portraits of transmasculine people in their authentic and personal lives. It really strikes me as a jaw-dropping coffee table book and a must-have for trans people.
