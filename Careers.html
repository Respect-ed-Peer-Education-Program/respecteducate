Board of Directors

We are currently seeking new board members to bring onto our team! Applications are open from now through June 1st.

Are you a young person with experience in sex education, reproductive justice work, sexual violence response, student advocacy, or other relevant work? Are you passionate and knowledgeable about consent and sexuality topics? Consider applying for a board position with Respect(Ed).

Preferred Qualifications:

  • We prioritize youth applicants (between 16 and 23 years of age).
  • Applicants should have at least 6 months of experience as a peer educator teaching about consent, sexual health, sexual assault prevention, bystander intervention, abuse prevention, and/or queer identity.
  • We strongly encourage people who have experienced sexual violence, queer and trans individuals, BIPoC, people with disabilities, and others with relevant lived experience/marginalized identities to apply.

Board Responsibilities:

  • Attend regular monthly meetings.
  • Review agendas and minutes, communicate with staff and other board members.
  • Vote on issues such as budgets, community partnerships, internal policy, etc.
  • Sit on additional Respect(Ed) committees as relevant to experience and interest (curriculum design, development, outreach, communications, etc).
  • Contribute a minimum of 4 hours per month to Respect(Ed) between meetings and other work.
  • Promote the Respect(Ed) mission.

Board roles are unpaid volunteer positions as dictated by law.

Advisory Board

We are currently in search of involved community members to serve on the Respect(Ed) advisory board. If you want to help support our mission by contributing your particular expertise and connections, consider joining!

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Advisory board members should have a professional background/expertise in education, sexual health, reproductive rights, sexual violence response, public health, development, or another relevant area.

Advisory Board Responsibilities:

  • Help raise resources, network, and community build.
  • Help us fundraise $2,000 per year.
  • Contribute ~12 hours of your time per year.

Advisory board roles are unpaid volunteer positions.
